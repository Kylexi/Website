ABOUT

Welcome to my portfolio. I am, among many things, a data scientist. I enjoy building/fine-tuning predictive models and working with raw data as a means to add value to businesses and individual lives. I'm also partial to programming (particularly automation), visualization, and teaching others.

However, I'm not just the nerd that can code in the basement into the late hours; I also take great pleasure in conveying and demonstrating complex ideas to people of all backgrounds. Knowing how to apply machine learning and statistical models is only half the battle; the other half (which is becoming more and more important as data science itself becomes accessible to all) is convincing others why conclusions are so.

For example, it doesn't do a mathematics layman any good when a modeler says "we're uncertain because your Bayesian prior wasn't informative and you had too little data". Instead, one should show the reasoning. To the left, you'll find my custom approach to conveying this kind of concept in a helpful, not-so-scary way. Check it out!

You'll notice that, as more data gets uncovered, in this case coin tosses to determine its "fairness", the distribution's peak becomes more and more pronounced (as does our certainty about the coin).
